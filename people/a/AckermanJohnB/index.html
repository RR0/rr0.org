Dans une réorganisation 1952-06, Ackerman devient Directeur Adjoint de la Collection & Dissemination of AF Intelligence, AFOIN-1. Il n'a pas de lien direct avec le projet Blue Book mais est particulièrement intéressé, d'après Edward J. Ruppelt, et a pour habitude de passer faire des visites. Selon Ruppelt, il a des idées definite as to what Blue Book had and what they should be doing. "He would tend to get all excited about individual sightings. He got copies of the UFO reports and several times he was on the phone wanting to know what we planned to do even before he had time to digest what was in the report." Ackerman had a "direct channel" to the top, to the Secretary of the Air Force and people in the Department of Defense.
