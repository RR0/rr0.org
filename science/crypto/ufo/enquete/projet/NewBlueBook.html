POUR DIFFUSION IMMEDIATE

LE PROJET BLUE BOOK, Le Bras Enquêteur de l'United States Air Force pour les Objets Volants Non Identifiés (OVNIs), a été réorganisé sous forme d'une association civile de recherche et d'enquête, composé de (mais pas limité à), d'ancien et actuel personnel de l'Air Force et d'autres agences du gouvernement U.S.. Le Nouveau Projet Blue Book, dont le nom a officiellement été approuvé par l'United States Air Force, étudiera les futurs signalements d'observations d'ovnis, mais se consacrera à d'autres études en profondeur de signalements d'observations plus anciens et classiques du milieu des années 1940 aux années 1960s.

Le Projet Blue Book d'origine a fermé ses portes en 1969, mais many of the investigators of the earlier reports found that the most important cases were then, and still are to this date, listed as "Unidentified." While some possible answers were made on many reports at the time, most "answers" did not fit the actual reported sightings, and were later proven not to be the objects reported by reputable persons, including those persons who were in high-level government positions when they made the reports. The "Answers" did not fit the reports.

Le Nouveau Projet Blue Book sera dirigé par l'industriel Bill Pitts, un enquêteur vétéran for all civilian UFO organizations and various governement agencies. A high-level research team will re-evaluate the older reports and endeavor to determine whether the objects reported have been solid metallic-like structure and could be extra-terrestrial. An hypothesis long considered as the predominate answer.

Dans une déclaration communiquée aux media, Pitts dit : "We will be seeking first-hand information, and materials, from individuals personally involved in the earlier saucer-era sighting reports, and will greatly appreciate receiving from any such person, or members of the immediate family, any copies of government-agency correspondence, sighting reports, photo's and movies, (ESPECIALLY ON PLANE-GUN CAMERAS, IF AVAILABLE)."

Also, FIRST-HAND KNOWLEDGE, OR PROOF, OF ANY ALLEGED UFO "CRASH RETRIEVAL" SITUATION WOULD BE APPRECIATED. Any such information that is received will be handled in strict confidentiality, and persons sending such materials will remain anonymous, and all materials will be returned in their entirety. Any person who may have been under "GAG-Restrictions" of the past, should come forward immediately, while they are still able to do so to assist us in this
investigative endeavor of the world-wide phenomenon, Pitts said.

Headquarters for the new Project Blue Book, will be at Pitts' office, 506 North 2nd Street, Fort Smith, Arkansas 72901. It is this address, which is centrally located in the United States, to which all materials should be sent.
