Clark, J. E.: "airship, California, 1915", Magonia Exchange, 2007-08-07

Alighted In Ventura

Flights of a mysterious airship over Ventura county have begun again. Last week C. S. White and J. W. Shillington thought that they saw an airship pass over about 9:30 p.m. going north.

Twice residents of Oxnard believed that they saw an airship a month ago. It is worthy of note that the reports of the aircraft come with moonlight nights. There are several theories as to the night flyer, but the most plausible are that he is a member of the army aviation corps from San Diego, or some aviator practicing in secret. Moonlight nights would be suitable for secret practice as the air is quiet and best adapted to flying.

The aeroplane alighted in Ventura yesterday morning according to the Ventura Post which says:

"Alighting at the race track at 5:30 o'clock yesterday morning, after awakening many Venturans with the peculiar buzz of its engines, a strange aeroplane was seen by citizens here. Miss Esther Ayers, daughter of Mrs. M. Ayers, witnessed the alighting of the machine, and other members of the family heard the sound of the engines. Miss Ida Rodrigues, who is employed by Mrs. J. F. Newby, likewise saw the strange visitor, while Mrs. Newby herself heard the sound of the engines.

"There is unusual interest attached to these flights at unearthly hours of the air strangers.

"Inquiry will be made of the government officials in charge of the aeroplane department of the army at San Diego, and a report made of the time and brevity of the visit of the aeroplane, although it is an absolute certainty that the visitor was not attached to the American aeroplane corps. Had any air ship left either Los Angeles or San Francisco on such a long flight, report would have been made of it.

"This is the second time within the past two months that Ventura county has been passed over by strange aeroplanes. But it is the first time that Ventura City has been visited. A month ago, about midnight, a party of Oxnard witnessed the flight of a celestial stranger. No explanation came of the matter at that time, and it was quickly forgotten as a remarkable incident but nothing more.

"The aeroplane yesterday morning hovered along the coast for several hours, moving back and forth at a high altitude.

"It would be quite premature at this time to attach any far reaching theories as to the identity of the strange flyer. As yet, however, there is not the faintest suggestion of an explanation. As has been said, had any flyer from Los Angeles, San Francisco or San Diego attempted such a flight, it would have been heralded far and wide in the press. That the stranger does not wish his visits known is shown by the fact that the trips are made at unusual hours."
