(Special Cable to The New York Times and Montreal Gazette.)

London, December 21 — M. A. Wetherell, a Central African big game hunter, has, according to the Daily Mail, found a definite spoor of an amphibian on the shores of Loch Ness, whence stories of a remarkable creature have emanated lately.

"It is a four-fingered beast." Mr. Wetherell says, "and has feet or pads about eight inches across. I should judge it to be a very powerful, soft-footed animal about twenty feet long. The spoor I found clearly shows the undulations of the pads and the outlines of claws or nails."

The spoor, Mr. Wetherell declares, was only a few hours old and bore evidence the animal had attempted to climb a gravel bank and slipped back.

Local residents who have been chaffed about "seeing things" were greatly relieved at Mr. Wetherell's disclosure.
