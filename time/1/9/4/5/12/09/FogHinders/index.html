Searchers Comb Area Of Flares

Mystery still shrouded the disappearance of five navy torpedo bombers, a navy seaplane bomber and 27 airmen aboard them as closing darkness last night brought an over-night cessation to an extensive air, sea and land search in progress since Thursday.

Low-hanging fog over both land and ocean hampered the operations of airplanes of many types thrown into the search by air installations of the area, nearly a score of surface craft, including the escort carrier Solomons, and land rescue crews, equipped with "alligators" and a "weasel," jeeps and mobile radio equipment.

Land foces concentrated on combing an area between Lake Kissimmee and the east coast, where Capt. J. D. Morrison, Eastern Air Lines pilot, reported seeing flares in two spots and flames in another. The searching units included an amphibious complement and planes from the Banana River Naval Air base, a "crash" squad from the Vero Beach Naval Air Station, and searchers equipped with truck-mounted "alligators" from Miami.
