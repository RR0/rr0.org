New York, 6 juillet-Alors que les appareils militaires des États-Unis ont scruté les cieux des régions côtières du Pacifique sud aujourd'hui sans résultat, des soucoupes volantes ont été signalées au-dessus du Canada pour la première fois.

Nombre de résidents de Walsenburg (sud-ôiest de l'Ontario) affirment avoir vu 2 grandes formations de disques illuminés survolant un large arc dans le ciel la nuit de samedi.

Dans le Dakota du sud, un pilote de la Garde Nationale déjà dans les airs a reçu l'ordre par radio de "démolir" un disque argenté avec une courte queue qu'un résident de Sioux Falls dit avoir vu foncer à travers le ciel quelques minutes auparavant.

Le pilote est parti dans la direction du disque signalé, mais a indiqué par radio qu'il ne pouvait le trouver.

Test on students

Sidney, 7 juillet.- Des corpuscules rouges passant devant la rétine de l'oeil pourraient être les "soucoupes volantes" qui déroutent actuellement l'Amérique.

Cela a été prouvé aujourd'hui dans un test mené sur 22 étudiants de l'Université de Sydney, par le professeur F. S. Cotton, professeur de physiologie.

Adressing 450 students, he drew attention to the rables messages referring to the fact that hundreds of persons in many parts of the United States has reported seeing flying saucers. The professor took the students outside the main building and told them to look at the sky about a mile away and concentrate their gaze on a fixed point white standing perfectly still. He told them to let him know if they saw any objects corresponding to those described in the rabled reports. Within 10 minutes 22 students reported they had seen "flying saucers."

A written report submitted by every student indicated that he had seen objects which they said were oval-shaped and bright, moving rapidly in the direction of the long axis and with a tendency for the path of their motion to be curved.

A remarkable feature of drawings submitted by each student was that they revealed three features. These were: Silvery discs with a short tail, discs swinging in an arc, and discs resembling a string of flying pearls.

Professor Cotton said tonight that the objects seen by the students were readily caused by the effect or red corpuscles passing in front of the retina. Normally people acquainted with the red-cell phenomena might not be prepared to trace the connexion, because frequently these red-cell effects revealed a large number of objects. When looking at a blue sky, with scattered clouds, however, a person could see only a small number of objects apparently darting across the blue.

Professor Cotton added athat readers could draw their own conclusions about the reports of the "flying saucers".
