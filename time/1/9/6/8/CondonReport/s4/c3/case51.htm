Cas 51 - Base aérienne de Vandenberg

Base de la Force Aérienne de Vandenberg (Californie)

5 Décembre 1963

Enquêteur : Hartmann

Home > Rapport Condon > Sommaire > Etude de cas photographiques

Résumé

During a daytime launch of a Thor-Agena rocket, several tracking cameras independently recorded a bright, star-like object apparently passing the missile. The object has been conclusively identified as Venus.

Contexte

  • Heure : 13 h 54 (PST)
  • Lieu : Complexe 75-1-1, Vandenberg AFB (Californie)
  • Données de la caméra : UFO clearly shown in films from site TS1O, with a 16mm Mitchell camera using a 12 in. lens (frame rate: 24 FPS). Two identical cameras with 6 in. lenses did not show the UFO. Certain other films are also alleged to show the UFO but were not examined.
  • Conditions météo : Deep blue sky with scattered thin clouds. On the film sequence that shows the UFO, the sky is clear, but from the other two sites, at that moment, thin clouds were present, through which the rocket was still clearly recorded.

Observation, informations générales

The sighting was reported by R. M. L. Baker (1) as an example of an unidentified object with potentially discriminatory tracking data. Baker had received a copy of the tracking film through contacts at Vandenberg (2), and subsequently brought it to our attention.
